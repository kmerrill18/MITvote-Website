Voting Safely During the Pandemic

It's important think about how you will minimize your risk of infection while you cast your vote. Many states have expanded vote-by-mail in response. Voting by mail allows you to cast your vote without actually going to the polls. Another way to minimize risk of infection is vote early, since polls are usually less crowded on ealry voting days. You can also vote in-person on election day with proper precautions. For more information on voting safely during the pandemic, check out this infographic from N95DECON on voting safely this fall. If you have any questions about voting in your state or changes to the normal process due to the pandemic, fill out THIS FORM and an MITvote team member will get back to you shortly!

Voting in MA

You can vote by mail, vote in person early, or vote in person on election day in MA.

Dates and Deadlines

  • Register to vote - received by October 24th at 8pm
  • Request mail-in ballot - received by October 28th at 8pm
  • Return voted mail-in ballot - received by November 3rd at 8pm

Vote by Mail

Legislation passed in July allows for no-excuse vote-by-mail in MA for the 2020 general election. This means that anyone can request a mail-in ballot without needing to specify a reason. You must request a ballot at least 4 business days before the election. The MA secretary of state office strongly recommends you request your mail in ballot as soon as possible. You can find a downloadable mail-in ballot application HERE. If you are registered to vote in Cambridge, mail your completed application to the Cambridge Election Commision at the following address:

CAMBRIDGE ELECTION COMMISSION
51 INMAN ST, FIRST FLOOR,
CAMBRIDGE, MA 02139

You can find contact information for all local election commissions in MA HERE. Make sure to request your mail-in ballot early! Lots of people will be requesting mail-in ballots due to COVID-19, and you want to be sure it gets to you in time. You can find more information about elections and COVID-19 in MA HERE. Email vote-exec@mit.edu with any questions!
