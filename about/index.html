About Us

MITvote is a nonpartisan group of graduates and undergraduates with the goal of engaging an active voter community on campus. Our mission is to make voting as accessible as possible to everyone and encourage civic participation on MIT's campus.

We organize voter registration drives, hold absentee ballots events, provide information on how to vote to students, and connect students with voter resources. We work to raise awareness of upcoming elections and educate students on how to participate in the civic process. We participate in activism surrounding voter issues and support legislation like same-day voter registration, expanding early voting, and non-citizen voting in municipal elections.

Current Projects

Virtual Student Engagement

Because of COVID-19, many of our usual tactics for student engagement are no longer possible. MITvote has been working to come up with the most effective plan to reach students virtually. We have been co-hosting office hours with various groups on campus to virtually provide face-to-face voter assistance. We have also created a voting questions form in collaboration with the UA so students can reach us via email with voting questions. Lastly, we are planning to reach out to every student individually via email about their voting plan.

Reaching First Years

MITvote has been working in the last year to engage first years in particular. We feel that if we show first years the importance that the institute places on civic engagement, voting will become a habit. We have been working with the Office of the First Year to make voter registration a part of official Orientation, as well as brainstorming other ways to reach first year students.

Getting Faculty Involved

We have been working to get faculty involved in encouraging students to vote. Faculty can act as another indicator of the importance the institute places on civic engagement as well as a reminder to vote in the academic sphere of student life

Liaison Program

MITvote has found that the most effective way to engage with students is through their peers. It is one thing for students to walk by our table, but it is another for a friend to ask, "Hey, are you planning to vote?". Thus, MITvote has been working to recruit students in each grad department and as many living communities and student groups as possible to act as a resource and reminder to their peers. For graduate students, we focus on each department. For undergrads, we focus mostly on living communities. We have been working with GRA's to act as liaisons to undergraduate living communities.

Since the spring, MITvote has been guest blogging for the PKG Center. You can find all of our posts on the PKG Center blog HERE!

BostonVotes - collaboration with other Boston-area schools

Last fall, MITvote joined a coalition of groups like us at other Boston-area schools. This coalition allows us to share ideas and resources between groups to advance the larger goal of student voter turnout. We work with them to advance common goals and demands for institutional support and voter turnout goals.

Reflections on 2019

After the 2019 election season, MITvote wrote a report detailing our activities in 2019, similar to our 2018 report. We found that while turnout decreased in most precincts in Cambridge, turnout increased in MIT precincts. Read the entire report HERE.

Reflections on 2018

After the 2018 election season, MITvote wrote a report detailing our actions leading up the election and our success. We found 196% increase in voter turnout on campus in the 2018 midterm elections over the 2014 midterm elections. If you would like to learn more about what MITvote has done in the past, what worked, what didn't and how voter registration and turnout changed in 2018, check out that report HERE!

Join Us!

MITvote needs people in all levels of commitment and involvement. We would love to have more regular attendees to help us plan and run events, poster, create voter materials, and help with administrative tasks. We are also currently recruiting students to act as liaisons to their communities. This role would include encouraing and reminding your peers to register and vote, as well as providing and the necessary forms and answering any questions they have. Lastly, we also need a healthy list of volunteers for staffing registration drives and events, which is more of a sign up as you can basis.

If you are interested in helping out, please email us at vote-exec@mit.edu, and we can tell you more about us. Being a regular member of MITvote generally requires about 1-2 hours a week of commitment. If you are interested in volunteering but you don't want the regular time commitment, please add yourself to our mailing list (mitvote-all@mit.edu) to receive emails with club info and volunteer opportunities. Thank you so much for your interest!

Feel free to email us with any questions you have about voting, getting involved, or anything else. We have weekly meetings, which you are welcome to attend.
